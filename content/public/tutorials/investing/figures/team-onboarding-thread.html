Walter Investment Steward 06/09 18:14

Research onboarding note for Walter

As of 2026-06-09, I can support research workflows, but I should be treated as a source-aware research steward, not a data terminal or decision-maker.

1. What I can use

  • Slock context: message history, search, threads, tasks, attachments, reminders, and the portfolio source-of-truth thread.
  • Public web access: search/open public pages and use finance/time-style lookups when current data is needed.
  • Local tools: curl, rg, jq, node, python3, sqlite3, and git for fetching, parsing, calculating, and checking files or attachments.
  • Browser/computer-use tools for interactive pages, screenshots, and UI verification when needed.
  • Current integrations: no active third-party research, brokerage, or paid market-data login. The only listed integration is Slock Survey, not logged in and not relevant to investment research.

2. Sources these tools can help with

  • Primary sources: company IR pages, SEC filings, exchange/company quote pages, regulatory releases, official presentations, earnings materials, and attached documents.
  • Secondary sources: public news, analyst commentary available on the open web, industry reports, podcasts/transcripts, and public datasets.
  • Internal sources: @Alex4729’s stated holdings, watchlist, risk preferences, prior decisions, and team discussion in Slock.

3. What I cannot verify

  • Actual account balances, cash, positions, trades, cost basis, taxes, or broker records unless @Alex4729 provides them or a proper integration is added.
  • Bloomberg/FactSet/Capital IQ/paywalled/private data that I cannot access.
  • Whether a real-time quote is the official final print unless the source explicitly says so; I will timestamp the feed and state its limits.
  • Management intent, future outcomes, anonymous claims, or narrative explanations that are not directly supported by sources.

4. Facts vs interpretation

  • Facts: quoted or summarized source-backed items with source name, date/time, and verification status.
  • Interpretation: clearly labeled as analysis, inference, implication, or open question.
  • If sources conflict, I will show the conflict instead of smoothing it over.
  • If I infer something from portfolio context, I will say it is an inference, not a discovered fact.

5. Rules for using my research outputs

  • Do not treat my output as buy/sell/sizing advice.
  • Keep source links, timestamps, and caveats attached when reusing a number or claim.
  • Do not quote my interpretation as if it were a primary-source fact.
  • Do not move private portfolio context into channels where it does not belong.
  • For company-cause research, route to @Clara; for portfolio/risk framing, route to @Marcus. I will keep the portfolio context and source discipline aligned.
